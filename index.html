Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Name: Muhammad Ali Jinnah
Born: December 25, 1876 – Karachi, British India
Died: September 11, 1948 – Karachi, Pakistan
Known For: Founder of Pakistan, Lawyer, Politician
About:
Muhammad Ali Jinnah, known as Quaid-e-Azam, was the leader of the All-India Muslim League and the driving force behind the creation of Pakistan in 1947. He was a visionary leader who fought for the rights of Muslims in the subcontinent and successfully secured a separate homeland for them. Jinnah served as Pakistan’s first Governor-General until his death in 1948. He is remembered as the "Father of the Nation" for his dedication, leadership, and unwavering determination.
Quote:
"With faith, discipline and selfless devotion to duty, there is nothing worthwhile that you cannot achieve."

Liaquat Ali Khan.

Name: Liaquat Ali Khan.
Born:October 1, 1895, in Karnal, British India (now in Haryana, India).
Died: October 16, 1951, in Rawalpindi, Pakistan.
Known For: Liaquat Ali Khan was Pakistan’s first Prime Minister (1947–1951) and a key leader in the Pakistan Movement alongside Quaid-e-Azam. He played a major role in shaping Pakistan’s early governance and foreign policy.
About:
Liaquat Ali Khan was Pakistan’s first Prime Minister and a close associate of Quaid-e-Azam. He worked tirelessly for the Pakistan Movement and played a crucial role in stabilizing the new nation after independence. His leadership in economic and foreign policy laid the foundation for Pakistan’s governance.
Quote:

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan

Name: Syed Ahmad bin Muttaqi Khan
Born: October 17, 1817, in Delhi, British India.
Died: March 27, 1898, in Aligarh, British India.
Known For: Sir Syed Ahmed Khan was a reformer, educationist, and philosopher. He founded the Aligarh Movement and Aligarh Muslim University, playing a key role in modern education for Muslims in India.
About:
Sir Syed Ahmed Khan was a prominent reformer and educationist who worked to modernize the Muslim community in India during the 19th century. He founded the Aligarh Movement and established Aligarh Muslim University to promote scientific education and social progress among Muslims. His efforts laid the foundation for intellectual awakening in the subcontinent.

Fatima Jinnah

Name: Fatima Jinnah
Born:31 July 1893, Karachi (British India)
Died:9 July 1967, Karachi (Pakistan)
Known For:Sister of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah and leading figure in Pakistan Movement.
About:
Fatima Jinnah, widely known as Māder-e-Millat (Mother of the Nation), was the younger sister of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah. She was a dental surgeon by profession but played a crucial role in the Pakistan Movement. Fatima Jinnah stood as a strong political figure, advocating for democracy and women's rights in Pakistan. After independence, she continued her efforts for social development and later contested the 1965 presidential election against Ayub Khan, becoming a symbol of resistance and integrity.
Quote:
“Women who can mould the character of the youth are more powerful than the men who run the affairs of the state.”

Begum Rana Liaquat Ali Khan

Name:Sheila Irene Pant
Born: 13 February 1905 in Almora, United Pronices of Agra and Oudh, British India.
Died: 13 June 1990 in Krachi, Pakistan.
Known For:Begum Rana Liaquat Ali Khan was the first First Lady of Pakistan, founder of APWA (All Pakistan Women’s Association), a prominent freedom fighter, and a champion of women’s education and rights. She also served as Pakistan’s Ambassador to several countries and became the first female Governor of Sindh.
About:
Begum Rana Liaquat Ali Khan (1905–1990) was a leading Pakistani stateswoman, social reformer, and the first First Lady of Pakistan. She played an active role in the Pakistan Movement, mobilizing women for relief and political activities during the struggle for independence. After 1947, she founded the All Pakistan Women’s Association (APWA) to promote women's welfare, education, and social rights. She served as Pakistan’s Ambassador to the Netherlands, Italy, and Tunisia and later became the first female Governor of Sindh (1973–1976). Her lifelong work focused on women’s empowerment, education, and social development, making her one of the most influential women in Pakistan’s history.
Quote:
“No nation can rise to the height of glory unless your women are side by side with you.”

Begum Shaista Ikramullah

Name: Shaista Suhrawardy Ikramullah.
Born: July 22, 1915, in Calcutta, British India.
Died:December 11, 2000, in Karachi, Pakistan.
Known For: Begum Shaista Ikramullah was the first Muslim woman with a Ph.D. from London, a leader of the Pakistan Movement, UN delegate, and author of “From Purdah to Parliament”.
About:
Begum Shaista Ikramullah (1915–2000) was a Pakistani politician, diplomat, and writer. She was the first Muslim woman to earn a Ph.D. from the University of London and played an active role in the Pakistan Movement. As a member of the Constituent Assembly, she signed Pakistan’s first constitution. She also represented Pakistan at the United Nations in 1948, advocating for human and women’s rights, and later served as Ambassador to Morocco. Her book From Purdah to Parliament highlights her journey and social reforms.
Quote:
“Education is the key to freedom and progress for women.”

Benazir Bhutto

Name:Benazir Bhutto Zardari
Born: June 21, 1953, in Karachi, Pakistan.
Died:December 27, 2007, in Rawalpindi, Pakistan.
Known For: First female Prime Minister of Pakistan (and the Muslim world). Twice elected Prime Minister (1988–1990, 1993–1996). Leader of the Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP). Advocate for democracy and women’s empowerment.
About:
Benazir Bhutto was a Pakistani politician and stateswoman who made history as the first woman to lead a Muslim-majority country. Educated at Harvard and Oxford, she became Prime Minister of Pakistan in 1988. Her tenure focused on modernization, women’s rights, and social reforms, though marked by political challenges. She was assassinated in 2007 during an election campaign rally, becoming a symbol of democracy and resilience.
Quote:
“Democracy is the best revenge.”
